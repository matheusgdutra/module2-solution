Baião de dois

Traditional dish originating from the Brazilian state of Ceará. The origin of the name is related to baião, a music and dance style, with the number 2 (dois) referring to the combination forming the base of the dish: rice and beans. Ingredients: Rice cooked with the beans, along with onion, tomato, pepper, chopped smoked sausage, bacon and jerked beef. Cheese and cream are also added.

Escondidinho

Traditional Brazilian casserole. The original one is called escondidinho de carne seca, made with a combination of dried and salted meat and mashed potatoes. The name of the dish means little hidden one. Jerked beef is cooked with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and various spices, then topped with mashed potatoes, and often grated cheese. The combination is baked in the oven until golden brown on top.

Panelada

Typical dish of the Brazilian Northeastern cuisine. For some, panelada is the ultimate comfort food. For others, even the smell of panelada cooking is enough to send them flying out of the kitchen. A proper panelada is prepared with the intestine, rumen and pieces of ox paws. It's these ingredients that make panelada, to coin a phrase, one man's meat and another man's poison.
